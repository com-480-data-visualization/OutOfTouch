Overview

Pre-Pandemic

Before the pandemic, there was a substantially high number of trips from one zone to another, indicating a robust and interconnected urban mobility network. Notably, East Village and East Chelsea emerged as critical zones, demonstrating significant activity and serving as key hubs within the city's transportation landscape. This highlights their importance before pandemic, showcasing their vital roles in the daily commute of the city's residents.

Pandemic

Compared to the period before the pandemic, we can observe a decrease in inter-zone trips, with a focus on intra-zone trips during the pandemic, leading to shorter trips. Another significant aspect is the overall reduction in the number of trips. Despite these changes, East Chelsea continues to be the area with the highest number of routes both to and from.

Post-Pandemic

After the pandemic, the behavior appears to have returned to its pre-pandemic state in terms of routes, with East Village and East Chelsea remaining central hubs of mobility. The number of trips has increased compared to the pandemic period.
